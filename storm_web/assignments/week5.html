WEBSITE CRITIQUE: Layout

1 GOOD Layout

I'm really in love with the graphic design of ID Magazine's home page. As a fashion magazine, ID is at it's core visual; the publication's brand is first and foremost based around beautiful, clean fashion photography. They translated this identity into these large, super wide photos stacked in a vertical column. Though you can't get an overview of the list of articles on their site, the large image size gives users the unique feeling of closeness and intimacy with the photo and thus the story. The navigation bar provides the utility missing from the minimal photo column as well as providing subtle, sitewide continuity.

2 BAD Layout

This website is so bad, yall. This site is just a bunch of boring links, and the lackluster color, styling, and layout only make the page feel cluttered. In turn, the clutter does not invite users to click on links and interact with the website. The letter tags and social media links at the top feel copied and not thought through at all.
